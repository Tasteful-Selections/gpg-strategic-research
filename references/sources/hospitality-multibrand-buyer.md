# Hospitality > Beverages for Multi-Brand-at-One-Buyer Problems

## When to Cite This Source

Any GPG research touching:
- Customer team design, key account orchestration, multi-brand presentation at retailers
- Matrix orgs serving single buyers across categories
- "How do we show up with the full portfolio" structural questions
- Cross-industry benchmarking on category/brand portfolio commercial design
- M&A target screening where the target's commercial structure needs evaluation against a multi-brand-at-buyer benchmark

## The Structural Match

Only hospitality has the structural problem of one corporate buyer purchasing across 25-30 brands from one company (e.g., a Goldman Sachs travel manager buying Marriott + Westin + Ritz + Sheraton + Courtyard + Residence Inn for one corporate travel program). That's the same structural problem as Walmart's produce buyer purchasing carrots + salads + organic + potatoes + value-added from GPG.

## Specific Named Architectures

- **Marriott:** Drew Pinto's Global Sales Office (GSO), 6,000+ associates managing $35B+ revenue across 30+ brands through Total Account Management — each major corporate client gets ONE Account Director who orchestrates the brand portfolio in the conversation
- **Hilton:** Chris Silcock as President, Global Brands AND Commercial Services — bundled C-suite seat
- **IHG:** Balsley bundling Commercial + Marketing
- **Brown-Forman post-Jan 2025:** simplified to one global CMO under regional commercial GMs (closest beverage analog but still distributor-mediated)

## Why Beverages Don't Fit

Three-tier distributor system means each spirits brand sells through different distributor portfolios at different retailers. Pull-through is distributor-by-distributor — not retailer-by-retailer. The brand-house/commercial-house split exists for different structural reasons than at GPG. Beverages was the obvious first guess but doesn't translate.

## Don't Forget

The bundled C-suite seat pattern (Hilton Silcock-style "President, Global Brands AND Commercial Services") is the dominant emerging architecture at multi-brand companies of GPG's scale. GPG already has this via Tim-under-Ross.

## Confidence

HIGH. Counterintuitive finding (beverages would have been the obvious first guess) makes this a high-value pre-cached reference for next similar research. Validated against three hospitality companies with strong public disclosure (Marriott investor day materials, Hilton 10-K, IHG annual reports).
