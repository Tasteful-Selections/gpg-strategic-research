# M&A Screening Framework

Tiered evaluation workflow for potential acquisition targets. Starts with quick screening, goes deep only when Jon signals.

## When This Applies

When Jon mentions acquisitions, "should we buy...", asks about a company as a potential fit, or wants to identify targets in a specific segment. Also when the PE firm's acquisition appetite comes up in strategic discussions.

## Workflow

### Tier 1: Quick Screen (Default)

The default depth. Produce this unless Jon asks for more. Takes 3-5 searches.

For a given company or segment, evaluate against GPG's three screening criteria (see `context/ma-criteria.md`):

1. **Revenue/scale fit** — Is this company the right size? Too small to matter, too large to swallow, or in the range where GPG could realistically acquire and integrate?
2. **Geographic/operational synergy** — Does this company's footprint complement GPG's? Overlapping geographies create cost synergies. Non-overlapping ones create expansion. Which is it?
3. **Category expansion opportunity** — Does this get GPG into a category or channel it doesn't currently serve? What's the strategic value of that category?

Output: A 1-paragraph screening brief per company. "Interesting because [X]. Concerns include [Y]. Worth a deeper look: yes/no/maybe, because [Z]."

### Tier 2: Evaluation Brief (On Request)

When Jon says "tell me more about this one" or "go deeper." Takes 8-12 searches.

Expand the screen into a structured evaluation:

1. **Company overview** — what they do, scale, categories, geography, ownership
2. **Strategic fit** — how the three screening criteria play out in detail
3. **Synergy potential** — where GPG + this company is worth more than the parts. Be specific: shared distribution, cross-selling, operational consolidation, category expansion.
4. **Risk factors** — integration complexity, cultural fit (family company vs. PE-backed vs. corporate), customer concentration, operational risks
5. **Open questions** — what you can't determine from public information that would need due diligence

Output: 1-2 page structured analysis. Can be HTML or Word depending on Jon's preference (check `references/output-formats.md`).

### Tier 3: Deep Research (Rare, Explicitly Requested)

Full research paper. Jon will be explicit when he wants this. Takes 15+ searches and should produce a comprehensive document.

Add to Tier 2:
- Detailed financial analysis (what's publicly available or estimable)
- Market position analysis in each category
- Customer overlap and concentration analysis
- Competitive reaction — what happens in the market if GPG acquires this company?
- Integration considerations — what would it take operationally?

Output: Full research paper via Word document. Include executive summary up front.

## Segment Scanning

When Jon says "find me targets in [category/segment]" rather than evaluating a specific company:

1. Define the segment criteria with Jon
2. Use web research to identify 5-10 companies in the space
3. Run Tier 1 screens on each
4. Rank by strategic fit
5. Present the top 3-5 with screening briefs

## Common Pitfalls

- **Going to Tier 2 or 3 without being asked.** Default to Tier 1. Jon will tell you when to go deeper. His time is the constraint, not yours.
- **Valuation opinions.** You can discuss scale and publicly available financial indicators. Don't estimate what a company is "worth" or what GPG should pay. That's banker territory.
- **Forgetting integration reality.** GPG is already consolidating 5 entities. Another acquisition adds to that complexity. Factor integration burden into the analysis.
- **Ignoring ownership structure.** Who owns the target matters enormously. A founder-owned company has different dynamics than a PE portfolio company on a timeline.
