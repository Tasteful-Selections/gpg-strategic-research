# Commercial Org Design at Produce Peers — Benchmarking

**Source-quality caveat up front.** Three of the six peers (Wonderful, Naturipe, Driscoll's) are private. Public visibility into their commercial org structures relies on press releases, leadership-page bios, LinkedIn role inference, and trade-press coverage of executive moves — not filings. Findings on those companies are partial and biased toward what the companies choose to publicize (typically marketing leadership). The two SEC registrants (Dole plc, Fresh Del Monte) and one (Mission Produce, IPO Sept 2020) yield more structured signal, but their 10-Ks describe operating segments — not commercial-function org design directly. Where I am inferring, I say so. Where I cannot tell, I say so.

---

## 1. Dole plc

**Commercial scope.** No single Chief Commercial Officer. Commercial functions sit within three operating segments — Fresh Fruit; Diversified Fresh Produce – EMEA; Diversified Fresh Produce – Americas & ROW — each with its own leadership and customer base. Group-level executives are CEO Rory Byrne, COO Johan Lindén, CFO Jacinta Devine, Executive Chairman Carl McCann. Commercial leadership is *embedded in segments*, not centralized. **Confirmed** (10-K segment disclosure).

**Entity/brand x function matrix.** Strongly **decentralized by segment/geography**. Each segment "is managed separately due to differences in geography, products, production processes, distribution channels and customer bases" (10-K language). Diversified Fresh Produce – EMEA encompasses 15+ country businesses (Ireland, Netherlands, Spain, Portugal, France, Italy, UK, Sweden, Denmark, South Africa, Czech, Slovakia, Poland, Germany, Brazil) — many retain local commercial teams from the legacy Total Produce federated model. Dole Food's North American Fresh Fruit business runs its own commercial leadership (separate marketing — Mike Secor as VP Marketing, Americas at Dole Packaged Foods is a different entity, the Itochu-owned packaged-food spinoff). **Confirmed** federation; **inferred** depth of country-level autonomy.

**Customer x category matrix.** Unknown at granularity required. Dole's North American banana/pineapple business has its own key-account team; vegetables (now divested) had separate. Within EMEA, country businesses run country-specific retailer relationships. No visible single key-account leader covering Walmart globally across all segments. **Inferred**.

**Channel splits.** Retail dominates; foodservice and wholesale handled within country/segment teams rather than a dedicated channel head. **Inferred**.

**Recent reorgs (last 3 years).** Major. (a) 2021 merger of Dole Food + Total Produce created Dole plc; integration of two very different operating models (centralized US vs. federated European) is ongoing. (b) **Fresh Vegetables Division divested** — Fresh Express deal terminated March 2024 over DOJ opposition; sold to organicgirl parent for $140M, completed late 2024/early 2025. (c) Progressive Produce 65% stake sold March 2024 ($120M). Portfolio is being narrowed to core fresh-fruit + diversified-produce trading. **Confirmed.**

**Vertical integration handling.** Fresh Fruit segment is vertically integrated (owned banana/pineapple plantations in Latin America) — Sales-Supply interface managed within segment leadership. Diversified Fresh Produce segments are predominantly trading/distribution rather than grown-supply, so the integration question is less acute there. **Inferred.**

## 2. Fresh Del Monte Produce

**Commercial scope.** **Split, not unified.** No single global CCO. Three separate senior commercial roles publicly visible:
- Danny Dumas — SVP Sales, Marketing & Product Management (North America)
- Chip Obracay — VP National Sales (North America), reporting up under Dumas (inferred)
- Deema Anani — Chief Commercial Officer, *Specialty Ingredients* (Dubai-based, reports directly to CEO Mohammad Abu-Ghazaleh; appointed March 2025)

The "CCO" title exists but is scoped to the Specialty Ingredients innovation business, not core fresh produce. North America commercial sits under Dumas. **Confirmed** (BusinessWire press release, leadership page).

**Entity/brand x function matrix.** **Geographic, not brand-centric.** Fresh Del Monte structures commercial leadership by region: SVP North American Operations (Ziad Nabulsi); SVP Europe & Africa (Gianpaolo Renino); SVP Colombia/Ecuador/Central America/Brazil (Jorge Pelaez); VP South America (Enrique Zamora); regional APAC roles. Each regional SVP carries both operations and commercial responsibility for that geography — sales/marketing not separated from operations at the regional level. The Del Monte brand itself is licensed/shared with Del Monte Foods (canned, separate company), so brand-portfolio complexity is lower than Wonderful's. **Confirmed** structure; **inferred** combined ops/commercial role at regional SVP level.

**Customer x category matrix.** Unknown. North American national accounts likely covered by Obracay's team across all fresh categories (bananas, pineapples, melons, avocados, fresh-cut), but no public confirmation of a single Walmart customer-team-leader role. **Speculative.**

**Channel splits.** Fresh-cut (Mann's, Del Monte Fresh Cut) and the Specialty Ingredients/innovation business each have separate commercial leadership; fresh whole produce is regionally led. Foodservice not visible as a separate function. **Inferred.**

**Recent reorgs (last 3 years).** (a) Mohammed Abbas promoted from EVP/COO to President & COO effective January 2026 — combined-role consolidation at the operating level. (b) New CCO–Specialty Ingredients role created March 2025 to drive innovation revenue. (c) Multiple regional SVP appointments in 2023–2024. Trend is *role creation*, not consolidation. **Confirmed.**

**Vertical integration handling.** Heavily vertically integrated (banana/pineapple plantations, ports, refrigerated shipping fleet). Regional SVPs combine ops and commercial — the Sales-Supply interface lives inside one regional P&L leader rather than a separate corporate function. **Inferred.**

## 3. The Wonderful Company

**Public visibility caveat:** Private. The Resnicks famously control disclosure. Most of the below is **inferred** from operating-company press releases and brand-marketing announcements.

**Commercial scope.** **No corporate CCO.** Structure runs through *operating-company presidents* who own brand P&Ls, supported by *corporate functional shared services*. Stewart Resnick is Chairman, President, co-owner; Lynda Resnick is Vice Chair for marketing & product development across the entire portfolio (signals centralized creative leadership). Adam Cooper is SVP Marketing at corporate level; Benal Serin (per 2024 trade press) is SVP Marketing covering the cross-brand portfolio (pistachios, POM, Halos, Seedless Lemons). **Inferred / Confirmed mix.**

**Entity/brand x function matrix.** **Hybrid — and the most informative model for GPG.**
- *Operating-company presidents* run brand P&Ls: Zak Laffite is President of Wonderful Citrus (covering Halos, Seedless Lemons, Sweet Scarletts grapefruit). Wonderful Pistachios & Almonds, POM Wonderful, FIJI Water, Teleflora each appear to operate as their own businesses with their own presidents/GMs — though current presidents of pistachios and POM are not cleanly disclosed in public press.
- *Marketing is centralized at Wonderful Agency* — the in-house creative agency (led by Chief Creative Officer Bobby Pearce) executes campaigns across all brands. Lynda Resnick personally oversees this. Brand marketing campaigns ("Hands Off My Halos", "Lemons With Seeds Are Annoying") are produced through one creative engine but presented as distinct brands.
- *Sales is brand-embedded* — each operating company runs its own sales force into retail, because the buyer at Walmart is different for citrus vs. nuts vs. juice vs. water.

**Pattern: brand-dedicated P&Ls + brand-dedicated sales + centralized marketing/creative.** **Inferred.**

**Customer x category matrix.** Each brand sells separately to the same retailer's category buyer (citrus buyer for Halos, snacks/nuts buyer for Pistachios, beverage buyer for POM/FIJI). Wonderful does not appear to run a unified key-account team — the categories don't share buyers anyway. **Inferred.**

**Channel splits.** Retail-led; foodservice presence less prominent. FIJI is the major foodservice/HORECA bet within the portfolio and runs its own commercial team. **Inferred.**

**Recent reorgs (last 3 years).** Dennis Woodside joined as President in 2022 (succession-planning move under Resnicks). Lynda Resnick remains highly active operationally despite age. **Confirmed.**

**Vertical integration handling.** Heavily vertically integrated — Wonderful Orchards (formerly Paramount Farming) supplies the brands. Operating-company presidents likely own the supply-commercial interface within their brand. **Inferred.**

## 4. Naturipe Farms

**Public visibility caveat:** Private cooperative. Lower disclosure than even Wonderful in some respects.

**Commercial scope.** Structurally constrained by ownership. Naturipe Farms is the *marketing/sales joint venture* of four berry growers: Hortifrut S.A. (Chile), Munger Farms, Naturipe Berry Growers (California), MBG Marketing (Michigan blueberries). The JV exists *because* the partners chose to centralize commercial scale — Naturipe is, in effect, the shared commercial function. Jim Roberts promoted to President November 2024, having been President of Sales — a sales leader running the company. Bob Hawk is Board Chairman. **Confirmed.**

**Entity/brand x function matrix.** **Centralized commercial as the design intent.** Naturipe is a single fresh-berry brand/sales channel for four grower-owners' production. Sales/marketing/category management are unified under Naturipe; supply comes from four owner-growers. There is *also* a Naturipe Foods (value-added/processed/frozen) division — Jason Bellan was VP & GM of that division as of 2022 (and per one search result is now CCO; this single source is weak signal). **Inferred** for Bellan's exact current title.

**Customer x category matrix.** Naturipe sells four berry types (strawberry, blueberry, raspberry, blackberry) plus value-added — the same category buyer at Walmart/Costco/Kroger covers all four. This argues for one unified key-account team, which is what a single sales-led JV would naturally produce. **Inferred.**

**Channel splits.** Fresh berries flow to retail and foodservice; value-added (Naturipe Foods/Vitafoods) is a separate commercial unit serving frozen/ingredient/processed customers. The two split makes sense — different buyers, different price/spec dynamics. **Inferred.**

**Recent reorgs (last 3 years).** (a) Jim Roberts promoted from President of Sales to President, November 2024 — sales leader becomes top operator. (b) Vitafoods value-added entity formed; Munger Farms joined ownership. (c) MBG Marketing CEO Brad Moorer died — owner-partner leadership turnover. **Confirmed.**

**Vertical integration handling.** Naturipe *is* the bridge between owner-growers and customers. The cooperative-JV model essentially externalizes the Sales-Supply interface into a contractual relationship between the JV's customers and its owner-growers. Demand planning sits inside Naturipe; supply commitment sits with each owner. **Inferred.**

## 5. Mission Produce

**Commercial scope.** **Single SVP Sales & Marketing.** Cheryl Hoefs joined October 2022 as SVP Sales & Marketing — covers retail, foodservice, wholesale (US) plus international export. Reports up to John Pawlowski (President & COO, becomes CEO at April 2026 annual meeting). Steve Barnard (founder) becomes Executive Chairman in the same transition. **Confirmed** (press release, leadership page).

**Entity/brand x function matrix.** **Functional/centralized.** Mission's leadership page lists only CEO, Executive Chairman, CFO, CLO, VP HR — *no separate brand or commodity-level commercial executives are publicly named on the corporate page*. The 10-K reports three operating segments — Marketing & Distribution, International Farming, Blueberries — but these are operational/financial reporting segments, not separate commercial leadership domains. Stephen Fink was promoted to VP North American Sales (covering retail/foodservice/wholesale); Hector Soltero is Sr. Director Sales Planning. The commercial team is one team selling avocados primarily, plus mangos and blueberries as adjacencies. **Confirmed**.

**Customer x category matrix.** Single sales force across product lines. Mango and blueberry don't yet have separate national-account teams — they're sold by the same reps as part of the avocado relationship. **Inferred.**

**Channel splits.** Retail/foodservice/wholesale all under VP North American Sales. International export is a separate commercial track. **Confirmed**.

**Recent reorgs (last 3 years).** (a) Hoefs hired Oct 2022 (SVP Sales & Marketing). (b) Pawlowski named CEO-elect (effective April 2026) — Barnard to Executive Chairman. (c) Mango and blueberry expansion driving organic commercial expansion rather than reorganization. **Confirmed.**

**Vertical integration handling.** Vertical integration is a Mission story — owned farming in Peru (avocados, mangos, blueberries) plus distribution centers globally. The 10-K's three-segment structure (Marketing & Distribution / International Farming / Blueberries) makes the supply-commercial interface explicit at financial level: Marketing & Distribution buys fruit from International Farming and third-parties. Internal transfer pricing exists. Pawlowski's combined CEO+COO history suggests integrated supply-commercial decision-making at the top. **Confirmed** (10-K).

## 6. Driscoll's

**Commercial scope.** **Regional CCO + global CMO + global CEO.** Soren Bjorn became global CEO January 2024 (had been President, Driscoll's of the Americas — the largest BU). Garland S. Reiter Jr. named CCO of Driscoll's of the Americas, February 2024 — note the *regional* scope. Reiter "orchestrates Sales Strategy and Execution, Market Expansion, Customer Development, Marketing, and Business Solutions" within the Americas. Jiunn Shih named first-ever *global* CMO, September 2025 — explicitly to build "one global brand with strong local resonance." Frances Dillard moved from VP Marketing (Americas, 14 years) to first-ever Chief Communications Officer. **Confirmed.**

**Entity/brand x function matrix.** **Region-led with overlay of global CMO.** Three regional business units (Americas, EMEA, Asia/China incl. Australia JV); each has historically run its own commercial leadership. Reiter's Americas-CCO scope is broad — he owns Sales + Marketing + Customer Development + Business Solutions for the Americas region. Shih's global CMO role is the new overlay layer building unified brand globally — in tension with regional autonomy. **Confirmed.**

**Customer x category matrix.** Driscoll's sells four berry types (strawberry, raspberry, blueberry, blackberry) — like Naturipe, the same retail buyer covers all four. Single Driscoll's customer team into Walmart/Kroger/Costco is the natural model. The "Sweetest Batch" premium tier is a brand-marketing layer rather than a separate sales structure. **Inferred.**

**Channel splits.** Retail-dominant (Driscoll's is the #2 produce CPG brand at retail per Circana 2024). Foodservice and ingredient channels exist but are not visible as separately led. **Inferred.**

**Recent reorgs (last 3 years).** Significant C-suite buildout: (a) Bjorn → global CEO Jan 2024. (b) Reiter → Americas CCO Feb 2024. (c) Dillard → Chief Communications Officer (new role). (d) Shih → first global CMO Sep 2025. Pattern: *layering global functional roles on top of regional BUs* — Driscoll's is moving from federated regional model to matrixed regional+global. **Confirmed.**

**Vertical integration handling.** Distinctive model: Driscoll's owns the *genetics/IP* (proprietary varieties) and the *brand*, but most production is grown by independent growers under exclusive license. The Sales-Supply interface is therefore a grower-relations function, not internal vertical integration. Reiter's CCO role explicitly includes "the value chain, from the genetic deployment of proprietary varieties to its connection and impact to building a brand for the consumer" — i.e., the supply side is built into the commercial role. **Confirmed**.

## Cross-Company Patterns

**Three archetypes emerge.**

**Archetype A: Regional/segment-led federation (Dole plc, Fresh Del Monte, Driscoll's pre-2025).** Commercial leadership lives inside regional or segmental P&L owners. Each region's SVP/President carries both ops and commercial responsibility. Strength: respects local market and customer differences (especially across continents). Weakness: no single throat to choke on global key accounts; brand inconsistency; duplicated functional capability. Driscoll's is now actively *layering* a global CMO over this — interesting tell that the federation model has limits when retailer global accounts and brand consistency matter.

**Archetype B: Centralized commercial as core design (Mission Produce, Naturipe).** One sales-and-marketing leader covers everything. Works because (a) the company is single- or narrow-category (avocados-plus-adjacencies; berries-only) and (b) the same retail buyer covers everything they sell. Naturipe goes further — the company *is* the commercial function for its grower-owners. Mission's Hoefs role is structurally simpler than anything at Dole or Del Monte.

**Archetype C: Brand-P&L federation with shared marketing creative (Wonderful).** Operating-company presidents own brand P&Ls and brand-dedicated sales forces, but marketing creative is centralized at Wonderful Agency under Lynda Resnick. Works because (a) brands sell into different category buyers (nuts, citrus, juice, water are not one buyer) so there's nothing to centralize on the sales side, and (b) creative-led marketing is where scale economies live.

**Common produce-specific tradeoffs.**
1. **Vertical integration creates the Sales-Supply interface as a top-of-house question.** Mission, Fresh Del Monte, Wonderful, Dole Fresh Fruit all bundle ops and commercial at regional P&L level rather than splitting them into a corporate Demand Planning function. Driscoll's pushes that interface into the CCO role explicitly. Naturipe externalizes it via the JV structure. None of the peers has a corporate "Supply & Demand Planning" function as visibly named as GPG's.
2. **Marketing centralizes more easily than sales.** Whenever the company has multiple brands or categories, marketing tends toward shared creative/brand teams while sales stays close to the customer. Wonderful, Driscoll's, Fresh Del Monte all show this asymmetry.
3. **Category Management as a distinct function is rarely surfaced publicly.** It's typically embedded inside Sales (Driscoll's Reiter wraps Customer Development into CCO; Mission has Sales Planning under Soltero; Wonderful integrates it into brand teams). Stand-alone Category Management directors aren't a visible pattern at this peer set.
4. **The single-CCO global title is rarer than expected.** Only Driscoll's (regional) and Fresh Del Monte (Specialty Ingredients only) use the title. The default is SVP Sales & Marketing or operating-company president — not Chief Commercial Officer.

**Implication for GPG consolidation.** Mission Produce's centralized SVP Sales & Marketing model is the closest analog to a single consolidated commercial function across formerly-separate entities, *if* GPG's product portfolio supports unified customer coverage. Wonderful's brand-dedicated-sales + central-marketing model is the closer analog *if* the legacy entities sell into different category buyers and need their own go-to-market. Driscoll's current trajectory — regional CCOs with a global CMO overlay — is the closest in-flight reference for a company actively wrestling with the same federation-vs-consolidation tension.
