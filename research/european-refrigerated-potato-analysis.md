## 1. Executive Context

### The Business Case

Tasteful Selections currently generates **500,000 CWT of conventional potato by-product** and **75,000 CWT of organic potato by-product** annually. This by-product is currently sold at **$0.02/lb**, generating approximately **$100,000/year** in revenue from ~50 million pounds of conventional product and ~7.5 million pounds of organic.

**Source:** Brian Klesmith email, March 13, 2026; confirmed in Brian's working Excel file ("Per Todd Jones 500,000 CWT or 50 million pounds or 1,250 Semi-loads; 75,000 cwt Organic 7.5 Million Pounds"). [CONFIRMED — internal GPG data]

The innovation team (Todd Jones, Celeste, Justin, Hugh, Greg, Brian) is evaluating whether European market data supports pursuing a **cleaner-label, healthier, refrigerated value-added potato line** — either organic or conventional — that would command significantly higher pricing than current by-product disposal rates.

### What Brian Is Looking For

Per the email, the specific European data requests are:
- Fresh potatoes vs. refrigerated value-added: side-by-side growth comparisons
- Weekly/unit sales by store or region
- Dollar sales and margins
- Price per pound
- Growth percentages
- Promotion impact
- Market shares per major producer
- Top-down SKU listings
- Pre or partially cooked penetration
- Top-down company comparisons in growth
- Price points per pound on fresh vs. value-added
- Organic trends

### Data Availability Reality Check

**Most of the granular data Brian is requesting (weekly unit sales by store, SKU-level share, promotion impact, margin data) does not exist in publicly available sources for the European chilled potato market.** The equivalent of Nielsen/IRI scan data in Europe is Circana (formerly IRI Europe) and NielsenIQ Europe, both of which are paywalled and not publicly available at this level of detail. The Euromonitor "Chilled Processed Food" report is the most likely source for country-level European chilled potato segment sizing but costs approximately $1,200-$2,500 per report.

This analysis compiles what IS publicly available with full source attribution and confidence levels.

## 3. European Chilled/Refrigerated Potato Segment

### 3.1 Critical Segmentation Issue

**The "chilled/refrigerated value-added potato" segment is NOT a standard market segmentation used by major European industry data sources.** Eurostat, EUPPA, Europatat, and FAO structure their data as:
- Fresh table potatoes
- Frozen processed
- Dehydrated
- Crisps/chips

Chilled/refrigerated is lumped into broader "processed" or reported only at the company level. **This means there is no publicly available standalone European market size figure for this segment.**

### 3.2 What We Can Determine

**European frozen potato market (the dominant processed segment):**

| Metric | Value | Source | Verification |
|---|---|---|---|
| European frozen potato market size | USD 17.57 billion (2024) | Market Data Forecast | SINGLE-VERIFIED (market research estimate) |
| Projected frozen market | USD 23.9 billion by 2033 (CAGR 3.48%) | Market Data Forecast | SINGLE-VERIFIED (projection) |

**Source:** [Market Data Forecast](https://www.marketdataforecast.com/market-reports/europe-frozen-potatoes-market)

**Chilled/refrigerated — best available proxies:**

1. One market research source states frozen and chilled potato products together represent approximately **25% of the total processed potato market**. [SINGLE-VERIFIED — 360iResearch/Credence Research]

2. Aviko claims **50% worldwide market share in chilled French fries** with total company turnover of ~EUR 600M across all products. [DOUBLE-VERIFIED — PotatoPro + Food Chain Magazine + Wikipedia]

3. **UK-specific data (best single-country breakdown available):** Chilled potatoes represent ~9% of total UK potato market by value. Full UK breakdown: Fresh 34%, Crisps 32%, Frozen 25%, Chilled 9%. [SINGLE-VERIFIED — AHDB Potatoes/Chilled Food Association, ~2018 data; **CAUTION: this data is approximately 6-8 years old**]

**Source:** [360iResearch](https://www.360iresearch.com/library/intelligence/processed-potato); [PotatoPro - Aviko](https://www.potatopro.com/companies/aviko); [AHDB Potatoes](https://potatoes.ahdb.org.uk/market-information-0/retail-market)

### 3.3 Market Size Inference

**The following is an INFERENCE, not a sourced figure:**

If the total European processed potato sector is ~EUR 9.8B (EUPPA), and frozen accounts for the vast majority, and UK data suggests chilled is roughly one-quarter to one-third the size of frozen — then the European chilled/refrigerated potato market could be in the range of **EUR 1-2 billion**. However, this extrapolation carries significant uncertainty because:
- The UK is likely more developed in chilled prepared foods than most continental European markets
- The 2018 UK ratio may have shifted
- "Chilled" definitions vary (fresh-processed chilled vs. freeze-chilled are different; see Section 9)

**I don't know the actual European chilled potato market size.** The Euromonitor "Chilled Processed Food" report is almost certainly the source that would provide this, but it is paywalled.

**I DON'T KNOW the specific growth rate for the European chilled/refrigerated potato segment.** No publicly available source breaks this out separately. The best proxies are:

### 4.4 Net Assessment

The European trend clearly mirrors what we see in the US: **fresh table potato consumption is in structural decline; processed/value-added is growing.** The growth differential between fresh (declining) and processed (mid-single-digit CAGR) is well-documented. This supports the directional thesis that value-added is where the growth is. However, I cannot confirm the specific growth trajectory of the chilled/refrigerated sub-segment within processed.

## 9. European Formats, Packaging, and Shelf Life

### 9.1 Critical Terminology: "Fresh Chilled" vs. "Freeze-Chilled"

Two distinct product categories exist in European chilled potato retail, and they are NOT the same:

1. **Fresh chilled (e.g., Peka Kroef, Parmentine, CelaVita):** Made from fresh potatoes, processed (peeled, cut, cooked/par-cooked), pasteurized, packaged under modified atmosphere or vacuum, and stored/sold refrigerated. Never frozen. Shelf life typically 14-28 days.

2. **Freeze-chilled (e.g., Lamb Weston EMEA):** Frozen products that are thawed and sold in the refrigerated case. Different manufacturing process, different cost structure, different shelf life characteristics.

**This distinction matters** for competitive analysis. When Brian says he wants a "cleaner labeled value-added refrigerated potato line," the European benchmark companies he should be looking at are the fresh chilled producers (Peka Kroef, Parmentine), not freeze-chilled operators (Lamb Weston). [CONFIRMED — verified from product descriptions across company websites]

## 11. Strategic Implications for GPG/Tasteful Selections

### 11.1 What the European Data Supports

1. **The directional thesis is valid.** Fresh potato consumption is in structural decline globally. Value-added/processed is growing. Europe shows the same pattern as the US — fresh declining, processed/value-added growing at mid-single-digit CAGRs.

2. **Clean label is commercially viable in chilled potatoes.** Parmentine's "Easy & Good" launch (20-day shelf life, no preservatives, pre-cooked, 6 varieties) and Peka Kroef's pasteurized vacuum products demonstrate that you CAN make clean-label refrigerated potatoes work. European consumers are ahead of US consumers on clean label adoption.

3. **There is a clean label gap in the US market.** The dominant US player (Bob Evans Original, 60% share) uses preservatives and soybean oil. No clean-label brand exists at scale. Reser's "All Natural" line and Bob Evans "All Natural Homestyle" are niche offerings within their portfolios.

4. **The "different story than USA producers" Brian wants IS the clean label story.** European producers have leaned into minimal ingredients, no preservatives, shorter shelf life, premium positioning. That is the differentiation vs. Bob Evans/Reser's.

### 11.2 What the European Data Warns Against

1. **CelaVita's bankruptcy is a material risk signal.** A 55-year-old company, former Benelux chilled market leader, backed by McCain for 12 years, sold to PE, bankrupt in 15 months. 10 potential buyers walked away. Chilled potato processing is capital-intensive and operationally demanding. Success is not guaranteed.

2. **Chilled potato is NOT the same as frozen potato.** The European market is overwhelmingly frozen. Chilled is a niche within a niche. The companies succeeding in chilled (Aviko, Peka Kroef) have been in it for decades with specialized infrastructure.

3. **Shelf life is the operational challenge.** European fresh chilled products have ~20 days shelf life. US refrigerated potato products use preservatives to achieve 60-90 days. GPG would need to either: (a) match European clean label with shorter shelf life (challenging for US distribution), or (b) find clean preservation methods that extend shelf life (Parmentine appears to do this through cooking/pasteurization methods). This is a fundamental operational question for the innovation team.

4. **The European market data Brian specifically wants (SKU-level share, weekly sales by store, margin data) simply does not exist in public sources.** If the team needs that granularity, they would need to purchase Euromonitor or Circana Europe reports (est. $1,200-$2,500+).
