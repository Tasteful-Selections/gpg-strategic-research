# M&A Screening Criteria

Reference material — what makes an acquisition target worth evaluating for GPG. Used by the M&A screening workflow in `references/ma-screening.md`.

## Primary Screening Criteria

These three criteria determine whether a target gets past initial screening. All three should show at least some positive signal. A target that's strong on one but blank on the other two is probably not worth pursuing.

### 1. Revenue / Scale Fit

Is the target the right size for GPG to acquire and integrate meaningfully?

- Too small: acquisition overhead isn't justified; the business gets lost inside GPG
- Too large: GPG can't realistically acquire it, or integration would overwhelm the organization
- Right-sized: large enough to move the needle on GPG's portfolio, small enough to integrate without consuming all management capacity

*Note: Specific revenue thresholds should be informed by the PE firm's parameters. Update this section when those parameters are available.*

### 2. Geographic / Operational Synergy

Does the target's footprint complement GPG's operations?

- **Overlapping geography**: Cost synergies through consolidation — shared distribution, combined procurement, facility rationalization
- **Non-overlapping geography**: Market expansion — access to regions or markets GPG doesn't currently serve
- **Operational fit**: Compatible farming practices, processing capabilities, or supply chain infrastructure that integrates without massive retooling

The strongest targets show synergy on at least one operational dimension, not just geographic adjacency.

### 3. Category Expansion Opportunity

Does this acquisition get GPG into a product, category, or channel it doesn't currently serve?

- **New category**: Entry into a produce segment GPG hasn't participated in
- **Value-add capability**: Processing, branding, or product development capabilities GPG lacks
- **Channel access**: Foodservice, DTC, international, or institutional distribution GPG doesn't have
- **Innovation platform**: Technology, IP, or product formats that could be applied across GPG's existing categories

## Secondary Considerations

Not screening criteria, but factors that elevate or downgrade a target that passes the primary screen:

- **Ownership structure**: Family-owned (relationship-driven, potentially complex), PE-backed (timeline-driven, cleaner process), public (transparent data, premium pricing), cooperative (unique dynamics)
- **Integration complexity**: How hard is this to absorb given GPG is already consolidating 5 entities?
- **Customer concentration**: Heavy dependence on one or two retailers is a risk factor
- **Management team**: Do they stay or go? What institutional knowledge walks out the door?
- **Regulatory considerations**: Antitrust concerns if GPG's market position in a category becomes too dominant

## What This File Doesn't Cover

This file captures screening criteria — what makes something worth looking at. It does not cover valuation, deal structure, financing, or due diligence methodology. Those are outside this persona's scope.
