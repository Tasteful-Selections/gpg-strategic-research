# GPG Capabilities Profile

Reference material — what GPG is, what it owns, how it got here, and what gives it a right to win. Use when evaluating strategic fit, expansion opportunities, or "could GPG do this" questions.

## Company Overview

Grimmway Produce Group (GPG) is a PE-backed consolidation of five legacy produce companies under common ownership by Teays River Investments. Headquarters: Bakersfield, California. 100% USA-grown produce. Operations in 13+ states.

**The five entities:** Grimmway Farms (carrots, founded 1960s), Cal-Organic Farms (organic vegetables, founded 1984), RPE (potato marketing/sales, founded 1971), Tasteful Selections (bite-size potatoes, founded 2010), San Miguel Produce (fresh-cut leafy greens, founded 1976).

**Consolidation timeline:** Grimmway acquired by Teays River (2020) → RPE acquired, consolidated with Tasteful Selections as Tasteful Partners (2021) → San Miguel Produce acquired (July 2024) → GPG formally announced (October 2025).

**CEO:** Jeff Huckaby. **CFO:** Kirsten Anderberg (appointed October 2025, also oversees IT). **Organic Veg President:** Katie Diesl. **Carrot President:** Eric Prophet. **Potato President:** Todd Jones. **CCO:** Ross (Jon's direct report).

## Current Categories and Scale

**Carrots** — World's largest producer. 10 million pounds processed daily. 40,000+ acres. Conventional and organic. Baby carrots, whole carrots, juice concentrate. Brands: Grimmway Farms, Bunny-Luv, Cal-Organic. Pioneer of the baby carrot market (1990). Full vertical integration seed-to-shelf.

**Potatoes** — 1.2 billion pounds sold annually (RPE). Year-round production. Bite-size/specialty potatoes (Tasteful Selections — category creator, grew bite-size from 1.5% to 18%+ of US market in 10 years). Conventional and organic. Proprietary potato flavor profiles. Chip potatoes (CSS Farms). Mixed model: vertical integration (Tasteful Selections) + brokerage/marketing (RPE). Brands: Tasteful Selections, RPE, Cal-Organic.

**Organic Vegetables** — Largest organic vegetable producer in North America. 65+ certified organic crop varieties. 50,000+ certified organic acres. Year-round supply. Brand: Cal-Organic Farms. Supplier of the Year recognition from Whole Foods, Albertsons.

**Fresh-Cut & Value-Added** — Cut 'N Clean Greens (first washed ready-to-use cooking greens, 1995). Jade Asian Greens (first washed wok-ready Asian greens, 2010). Organic chopped greens and blends. Acquired via San Miguel Produce. Oxnard, CA facility.

## Operational Advantages

**Vertical integration:** Controls supply chain from seed/field through processing and packing to retail shelf. Most competitors rely on third parties for at least one link. Provides cost control, quality control, speed-to-shelf.

**Scale:** Dominant position in carrots (largest globally) and organic veg (largest in North America). Significant in potatoes. Scale creates cost advantages, retailer leverage, and year-round supply capability.

**Multi-state production:** California (primary), Arizona, Colorado, Florida, Georgia, Oregon, Washington, Texas, Nebraska, South Dakota, Wisconsin, Idaho. Processing facilities in CA (Bakersfield, Oxnard, Arvin, Tehachapi), WA, GA, WI. Enables geographic coverage and year-round growing seasons.

**National retail relationships:** Established accounts with major national retailers across all categories. Distribution infrastructure for nationwide reach. These relationships are the channel for any new product or category.

**PE backing:** Teays River Investments actively pursuing acquisitions for strategic growth. Capital and appetite that most produce competitors (family-owned, smaller PE) lack.

**Innovation track record:** Pioneered baby carrots (1990), created bite-size potato category (2010, grew from 1.5% to 18%+ market), first washed ready-to-use greens (1995), first wok-ready Asian greens (2010). Proprietary potato varieties. This is not a commodity-only company — it has a history of category creation.

**Brand portfolio:** Multiple brands across premium and value segments — Grimmway Farms, Cal-Organic, Bunny-Luv, Tasteful Selections, Cut 'N Clean Greens, Jade Asian Greens, RPE.

## Integration Context

GPG is actively integrating five legacy entities with different ERP systems, data architectures, operational practices, customer relationships, brand identities, and organizational cultures. Each legacy company has distinct heritage — California carrot farming (Grimmway), organic pioneering (Cal-Organic), Midwest potato/family farming (RPE/Wysocki), category creation (Tasteful Selections/CSS Farms), Japanese-American family farming and fresh-cut innovation (San Miguel).

Integration is consuming significant management attention and operational capacity. Any expansion opportunity competes with this work for resources.

## Capability Gaps to Consider

**Brand as consumer brand:** GPG is primarily known as a supplier/grower. Individual brands (Cal-Organic, Tasteful Selections) have some consumer recognition, but GPG itself is not a consumer-facing brand. Branded product strategies beyond current brands may require marketing investment GPG hasn't historically made.

**Value-added processing depth:** Current processing is oriented around fresh, cut, and washed produce. More complex value-added products (meal kits, heavily processed items, prepared foods) would require different infrastructure and capabilities.

**DTC/e-commerce:** Current distribution is B2B to retailers. Direct-to-consumer requires different logistics, marketing, and customer service capabilities.

**International:** Primarily US domestic. International expansion involves trade compliance, different retail structures, and supply chain complexity.

**Foodservice:** Some presence but primarily a retail-focused operation. Foodservice has different packaging, pricing, distribution, and relationship dynamics.
