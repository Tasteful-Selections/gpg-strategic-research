## Landscape Summary

**Total companies mapped:** 28 across 4 segments.

### Key Themes (March 2026)

**Direct competitors are consolidating.** Bolthouse split its fresh/beverage businesses. Dole FV sold for $140M to Arable/organicgirl. Fresh Del Monte is exiting fresh veg (sold Mann Packing to Church Brothers, sold Fresh Leaf Farms). The direct competitive set is reshaping — fewer but more focused competitors.

**Taylor Farms is the scale benchmark.** At $7B+ and 22 facilities, Taylor Farms (with Earthbound) dwarfs everyone else in value-added fresh produce. Not a target — a north star for what integrated scale looks like.

**Value-added is where the margin is.** Companies commanding premium pricing (NatureSweet, Little Leaf, Gotham Greens, Pero) have built brands around convenience and quality. GPG has the production base but hasn't fully pursued branded value-add beyond current portfolio.

**CEA had a brutal shakeout.** Over $3B in venture capital destroyed across Bowery, AppHarvest, Plenty, and AeroFarms. Survivors pivoted to niches (microgreens, premium strawberries) or consolidated (80 Acres + Soli Organic). The "buy distressed CEA assets" opportunity is real — Mastronardi already executed this playbook.

**Potential M&A-relevant names:** Church Brothers (post-Mann, expanding), D'Arrigo Brothers (right-sized, legacy brand), Duda (family-owned GPG analog), Windset (small CEA), Little Leaf (CEA lettuce leader), Local Bounti ($49M market cap), Revol Greens (distressed), AeroFarms (exploring sale).

**Technology partnership opportunity:** Apeel Sciences' shelf-life extension is directly applicable to carrots. Company is leaner and potentially more open to deals.
