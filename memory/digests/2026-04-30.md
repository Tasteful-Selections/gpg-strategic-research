# Session Digest — 2026-04-30

## What Happened
Cross-industry org design research commissioned to stress-test the Apr 29 commercial reorg working doc from outside food/ag/CPG. 7 research files produced covering 13 companies across 5 clusters: produce peers, CPG rollups, PE-backed food, multi-brand beverages + hospitality, and trading + production hybrids + multi-segment protein. Findings folded into the working doc at `C:/Repos/gpg-bi-strategy/deliverables/commercial-reorg-design-2026-04-29.md` as Section 13 with surgical insertions to existing Sections 1, 5.1, 7.2, 9, and 10. ## Decisions Made
- **Cross-industry research scope: 13 companies, 5 clusters** — not just food/ag, but also beverages/hospitality (Marriott, Hilton, Diageo, Pernod) and commodity trading (Cargill, ADM, Bunge, Glencore, Tyson, Sysco). Rationale: GPG's structural problems aren't fully addressed by food benchmarks alone.
- **Three structural additions recommended into the working doc**: Customer Team Leader / Total Account mandate for Kevin Wright (Marriott pattern, formalize what's already happening); VP RGM seat under Drew (universal gap, never implemented at GPG, likely external hire); channel-defined sub-VPs under Lisa (Pilgrim's pattern, pressure-test).
- **One critical governance addition**: Finance/Controlling-led transfer pricing committee under Kirsten (CFO) for RPE↔owned-supply transactions. CSCO is plant-ops only and not the natural owner. Driven by ADM Jan 2024 SEC settlement cautionary tale.
- **CCSO bundle question revised**: Wayne-Sanderson model doesn't apply cleanly at GPG because GPG's CSCO scope is narrowly plant-operations. Apr 29 design's split (CCO + plant-ops CSCO + sourcing pulled into commercial under Craig) is structurally cleaner than bundling.

## Key Discoveries
- **Hospitality is the closest cross-industry analog to GPG's "5 entities at Walmart" problem** — not beverages. Spirits sell through three-tier distributors, so brand pull-through is distributor-by-distributor, not retailer-by-retailer. Marriott's Total Account Management (one Account Director per major client orchestrating 30+ brands) is the direct structural template.
- **GPG already has the Hilton Silcock bundled brand+commercial seat** — Tim-under-Ross is steady-state architecture, not transitional. Reframe of earlier "future watch" framing.
- **CCO title is statistically unstable as steady-state architecture** — eliminated at B&G (Aug 2024) and TreeHouse (May 2025), split at Conagra, bundled into CCSO at Wayne-Sanderson. Two of three large rollup benchmarks eliminated within ~3 years of creation.
- **Sovos S-1 documents the cleanest PE-backed multi-entity architecture** — integrated sales / federated brand-GMs. Brand-GM layer doesn't translate to GPG's weaker brand differentiation; category-president organization (Eric/Katie/Todd) is the right adaptation.
- **ADM Jan 2024 transfer-pricing scandal is direct risk profile match** to GPG's RPE↔Tasteful interface under Craig. $40M SEC settlement, $228M restatement, executive comp clawback. Governance overlay (committee under Kirsten) is the mitigation.
- **Sysco's quantified cross-sell math (3x with FreshPoint, 6x with specialty meat)** is the proof-point metric that justifies the consolidation to Teays. GPG is already cross-selling but measurement infrastructure is clumsy due to ERP fragmentation pre-GIS unification.
