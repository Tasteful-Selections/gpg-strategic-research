# The Load-Bearing Question Pattern

## Context

Apr 29-30 commercial reorg org-design benchmarking session. After delivering a cross-industry synthesis with structural recommendations and a standalone appendix to the user's existing working doc, asked: "Any other questions you have based on the research you did to edit these files?"

Framed the response as: "Curated to ones that would actually change the appendix or sharpen recommendations, not just satisfy curiosity. The questions worth asking after research are the ones where my placeholder guess is doing structural load-bearing work."

Presented seven questions where my guess could be wrong in load-bearing ways. Jon answered all seven tersely (numbered, brief). Three of the seven materially changed recommendations:
- Q3 (Drew's team is already cross-category) downgraded the Total Account Management recommendation from "build new behavior" to "formalize what's already happening"
- Q4 (RGM never implemented at GPG) confirmed it as the only true net-new structural addition
- Q2 (CSCO is plant-ops only) revised the transfer-pricing committee owner from CSCO to Kirsten (CFO) and made the Wayne-Sanderson CCSO bundle question moot

## The Learning

After delivering a benchmarking synthesis with recommendations, ask the user about **load-bearing assumptions specifically** — questions where my placeholder guess does structural work in the recommendations — rather than open-ended "what should I check" or "any feedback."

### Why this works better

- **Focuses the user on what matters:** Load-bearing assumptions are the small number of facts where being wrong invalidates a recommendation. Curiosities are facts that would be nice to know but don't change the conclusion.
- **Time-efficient for the user:** A senior leader can answer "is X structurally true?" in 1-2 words. Open feedback requests get vague responses or are skipped.
- **Forces the synthesizer to surface implicit guesses:** The discipline of identifying load-bearing assumptions in your own recommendations is itself a quality-control step.

### The specific framing that worked

"Questions where my placeholder guess is doing structural load-bearing work."

Then for each question, brief framing: "If [my placeholder guess] is wrong, then [recommendation] needs to change because [structural dependency]."

Example from this session: "Q3: Does Kevin Wright actually have cross-category access at Walmart today, or do Eric/Katie/Todd each have their own Walmart buyer relationships that bypass Drew's team? The Marriott TAM recommendation assumes Kevin's role can be chartered as an orchestrator. If division presidents currently have direct buyer relationships outside Kevin's team, the recommendation is asking for those relationships to be subordinated — much harder political move."

### Format of the response

- Number the questions (Q1-QN, max 7-8) so the user can answer "1: Yes; 2: No; 3: under X; ..."
- For each, ONE sentence on what would change if my guess is wrong
- Group "questions I'm NOT asking" at the end with rationale (signals discipline; reassures user you're not just dumping research curiosities)

## When to Apply

- After any benchmarking synthesis that produces recommendations to be acted on
- After any market-mapping, M&A target screening, or strategic analysis where conclusions depend on real-world facts you've inferred
- Before producing a final deliverable on time-sensitive recommendations
- Anytime considering "should I deliver this or check first?" — load-bearing questions are the way to check

## Don't Apply When

- The deliverable is informational only (no recommendations to act on)
- The user has explicitly said "just deliver, don't check"
- You have direct access to ground truth (e.g., reading internal docs)
- The synthesis is preliminary scoping, not actionable recommendations

## Confidence

HIGH. Validated this session — Jon's answers to load-bearing questions changed three recommendations that would otherwise have been wrong. The framing ("load-bearing" specifically) was Jon-resonant — he answered all seven questions tersely without pushback, and explicitly directed me to "go ahead and build the appendix" once he'd addressed them.
