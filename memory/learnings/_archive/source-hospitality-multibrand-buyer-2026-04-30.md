# Hospitality > Beverages for Multi-Brand-at-One-Buyer Problems

## Context

Cross-industry org design benchmarking for GPG's commercial reorg (Apr 29, 2026 working doc). Initial expectation was that beverages (Constellation, Diageo, Brown-Forman, Pernod, Gallo) would be the best analog because of vertical integration + multi-brand portfolio similarities to GPG. Research revealed beverages sell through the three-tier distributor system, so brand pull-through happens distributor-by-distributor — not retailer-by-retailer. Hospitality (Marriott, Hilton, IHG) was the surprising-but-cleaner fit.

## The Learning

For "multi-brand portfolio at single retail/corporate buyer" structural questions, hospitality is a closer cross-industry analog than beverages.

**Why:** Only hospitality has the structural problem of one corporate buyer purchasing across 25-30 brands from one company (e.g., a Goldman Sachs travel manager buying Marriott + Westin + Ritz + Sheraton + Courtyard + Residence Inn for one corporate travel program). That's the same structural problem as Walmart's produce buyer purchasing carrots + salads + organic + potatoes + value-added from GPG.

**Specific named architectures worth referencing:**
- Marriott: Drew Pinto's Global Sales Office (GSO), 6,000+ associates managing $35B+ revenue across 30+ brands through Total Account Management — each major corporate client gets ONE Account Director who orchestrates the brand portfolio in the conversation
- Hilton: Chris Silcock as President, Global Brands AND Commercial Services — bundled C-suite seat
- IHG: Balsley bundling Commercial + Marketing
- Brown-Forman post-Jan 2025: simplified to one global CMO under regional commercial GMs (closest beverage analog but still distributor-mediated)

**Why beverages don't fit:** Three-tier distributor system means each spirits brand sells through different distributor portfolios at different retailers. Pull-through is distributor-by-distributor. The brand-house/commercial-house split exists for different structural reasons than at GPG.

## When to Apply

- Any GPG research touching customer team design, key account orchestration, multi-brand presentation at retailers
- Matrix orgs serving single buyers across categories
- Any "how do we show up with the full portfolio" structural question
- Cross-industry benchmarking on category/brand portfolio commercial design
- Future M&A target screening where the target's commercial structure needs evaluation against a multi-brand-at-buyer benchmark

## Confidence

HIGH. Counterintuitive finding (beverages would have been the obvious first guess) makes this a high-value learning to surface early on next similar research. Validated against three hospitality companies with strong public disclosure (Marriott investor day materials, Hilton 10-K, IHG annual reports).

## Don't Forget

The bundled C-suite seat pattern (Hilton Silcock-style "President, Global Brands AND Commercial Services") is the dominant emerging architecture at multi-brand companies of GPG's scale. GPG already has this via Tim-under-Ross.
