# GPG Dominant Market Share — Pricing Data Caution

## Context

Jon corrected the first GroundTruth Market Brief prototype, which included a 177% grower price increase over 5 years, FOB pricing tables, and Mexico-California price differentials. When GPG is 60-80% of supply, this data is negotiating ammunition for the buyer.

## The Learning

**Never include the following in buyer-facing materials:**
- Grower price-received trends ($/CWT over time)
- FOB shipping point price tables or YoY percentage changes
- Price differentials between origins (Mexico vs. California)
- Any framing that implies prices rose because of supply concentration
- Production value growth figures that imply price-driven increases (e.g., "$747M to $2B while volume declined")

**These are fine for internal analysis** (board supplements, Ross briefings, strategy docs). They are NOT fine for anything a buyer might see.

**Supply dynamics can be discussed neutrally:** water conditions, seasonal transitions, import patterns, weather — these are informational and useful for buyer procurement planning. Just don't attach price data to them.

## When to Apply

Every GroundTruth product, every buyer-facing deliverable, every category review. Also applies to any research output that might eventually be shared externally. When in doubt, ask: "If a buyer read this, would it give them leverage in a pricing negotiation?"
