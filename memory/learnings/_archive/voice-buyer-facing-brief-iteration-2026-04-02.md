# Buyer-Facing Briefs: The Three-Version Pattern

## Context

First GroundTruth Market Brief prototype (Carrots Q1 2026) went through three distinct versions before landing on the right tone and structure.

## The Learning

Buyer-facing category intelligence has three failure modes:

1. **Market analyst mode** (V1): Comprehensive data but includes information that gives the buyer negotiating leverage. When GPG is 60-80% of carrot supply, showing 177% price increases and FOB pricing tables tells the buyer "you drove our costs up." Strip all pricing power data from buyer-facing materials.

2. **Sales pitch mode** (V2): All the reasons to buy more carrots — demographic trends, organic growth, nutritional positioning. Too one-directional and transparent to be a credible recurring update. A buyer won't open a monthly email that's obviously a sales pitch.

3. **Trusted update mode** (V3 — correct): "Here's what's happening in your category that you should know." Covers the full product mix (not just GPG's focus segments), includes short-term dynamics (weather, supply transitions), has a rotating consumer spotlight, and offers light actionable context without prescribing. The buyer opens it because it's genuinely useful, and GPG benefits because the buyer thinks "this is the supplier who understands my category."

**The structural markers of V3:**
- Full product mix coverage (baby, organic, whole, bulk, value-added)
- Weather/growing region conditions section — biggest short-term supply variable
- Consumer spotlight as a rotating feature (one per issue, not a demographic thesis)
- Short-term market dynamics that affect procurement decisions
- "What to Watch" forward-looking section
- No pricing tables, no YoY price increase percentages, no FOB data
- No multi-row recommendation tables — one actionable implication per section

## When to Apply

Any GroundTruth deliverable intended for recurring distribution (email, portal) to retail buyers. Sales decks for specific meetings can be more directional — the V3 rules apply to the recurring products, not one-time presentations.

## Confidence

HIGH — Jon explicitly corrected V1 and V2 with specific reasons. V3 was confirmed as the right approach.
