# Wayne-Sanderson + Sovos as Canonical Benchmarks

## Context

Cross-industry org design benchmarking for GPG's commercial reorg. Across 13 companies in 5 clusters (produce peers, CPG rollups, PE-backed food, beverages/hospitality, trading/multi-segment protein), two emerged as the highest-leverage references for GPG's specific situation.

## The Learning

**Wayne-Sanderson Farms** and **Sovos Brands** are the two canonical benchmarks for any future GPG research touching commercial org design, integration sequencing, M&A integration, or PE-backed multi-entity rollup architecture. Use as named references rather than re-researching from scratch.

### Wayne-Sanderson Farms (Cargill + Continental Grain JV, June 2022 close, Apr 2026 reorg)

**Why canonical:** Closest structural analog to GPG. Multi-entity ag merger (Wayne Farms + Sanderson Farms), commodity-adjacent vertical integration, PE/JV-backed, completed integration ~4 years post-close. Cleanest example of:
- **Integration leader → permanent CCO pattern** — Aaron Leach (21-year Wayne insider, Strategic Planning → Supply Chain → integration leader 2022-2026 → CCSO Apr 2026)
- **Bundled Chief Commercial AND Supply Chain Officer (CCSO)** — single C-suite seat for fast-cycle ag commodity businesses
- **Two-channel split underneath the CCO** — SVP Retail (David Gadd) + SVP Commercial Sales/foodservice (Randy Meyers)
- **No CMO seat** — appropriate for commodity-fresh poultry; GPG should question whether absence translates given Bunny-Luv/Cal-Organic brand equity
- **4-year integration timeline as realistic upper bound**

### Sovos Brands (Advent International 2017-2024, IPO Sept 2021, Campbell acquisition Aug 2023)

**Why canonical:** Only PE-backed multi-entity food platform with S-1-grade public disclosure of commercial architecture. Cleanest example of:
- **Integrated-sales / federated-brand-GMs architecture** — single shared sales force across Rao's, Michael Angelo's, noosa, Birch Benders under Risa Cretella (CSO); brand GMs ran each brand's P&L
- **CSO promoted from inside (Cretella from EVP Group GM Rao's)** — credibility on brand side + channel/operations expertise
- **Centralized RGM/CatMan/Insights under the CSO** — Louisville-based Insights team supporting Category Management across brands
- **No corporate CMO** — brand marketing federated by brand-GM
- **Birch Benders divestiture pattern** — divested at year-2 (Dec 2022) when fit failure recognized; lost ~2 years of platform energy first
- **Survived strategic acquisition** — Cretella's role at Campbell (SVP & GM Distinctive Brands → EVP & President Meals & Beverages) is structurally identical to her Sovos role

## When to Apply

Any future GPG research touching:
- Commercial organization design at Ross's level or below
- Integration sequencing for new acquisitions or remaining legacy entities
- M&A target evaluation (especially how the target's commercial structure would integrate into GPG)
- PE-backed rollup architecture questions
- Strategic fit pre-screens (Sovos Birch Benders divestiture pattern)
- Integration-leader-as-CCO succession planning
- Brand-GM vs. category-president structural decisions

## What's Different at GPG

- **Brand-GM layer doesn't translate** from Sovos because GPG's brand differentiation is weaker (Cal-Organic / Bunny-Luv / Tasteful Selections is not Rao's / noosa / Michael Angelo's). GPG's category-president organization (Eric/Katie/Todd) is the right adaptation.
- **CCSO bundle doesn't translate** from Wayne-Sanderson because GPG's CSCO scope is plant-operations only, not full supply chain. Bundling Ross + CSCO would be paper exercise.
- **4-year integration timeline at Wayne-Sanderson is reality check** — don't promise PE faster without explicit structural case. Teays plans long hold per Jon, so internal forcing function needed.

## Confidence

HIGH. Both companies have strong public disclosure (Wayne-Sanderson press releases on leadership appointments confirm structure; Sovos S-1 + post-IPO 10-Ks + earnings transcripts document architecture). Findings triangulated against multiple sources.
