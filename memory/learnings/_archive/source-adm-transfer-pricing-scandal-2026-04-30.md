# ADM Transfer-Pricing Scandal as Named Governance Cautionary Tale

## Context

Cross-industry research on trading + production hybrid org structures (Cargill, ADM, Bunge, Glencore, Tyson, Sysco). Surfaced ADM's January 2024 SEC settlement as the documented failure mode for the merchant + owned-supply hybrid configuration — directly applicable to GPG's RPE potato merchant book sitting alongside Tasteful Selections owned-supply potato.

## The Learning

ADM's January 2024 transfer-pricing scandal is the direct cautionary tale for any GPG research touching:
- The RPE merchant book and its governance interface with owned-supply potato (Tasteful Selections, contracted retail programs)
- Any future merchant/brokerage business inside GPG (e.g., if GPG acquires another grower-brokered category)
- Intersegment economic transactions of any kind
- PE-backed governance hygiene questions

### Specific facts to cite

- **Date:** January 2024 disclosure of SEC investigation; settlement Aug 2024
- **The transactions:** Ag Services segment sold internally to the Nutrition segment at non-market prices, inflating Nutrition's reported profitability
- **Financial impact:** $40M SEC settlement; $228M in profit restatements across Nutrition for 2018-2023; ~$70M in executive equity bonuses tied to inflated Nutrition performance
- **Personnel impact:** Departure of Nutrition CFO and other executives
- **Subsequent restructure:** Two-thirds of Nutrition brands cut, 17% of products eliminated, 20+ animal nutrition production lines closed
- **Lasting damage:** Reputational damage and credit-rating pressure

### The structural cause

One executive layer over both upstream supply and downstream commercial WITHOUT independent transfer-pricing governance. Same configuration applies wherever a single executive (or executive layer) sets pricing on both sides of an intersegment transaction.

### The mitigation

Best practice from Glencore, Bunge, post-scandal ADM:
1. Finance/Controlling-led transfer pricing committee (NOT under the commercial line)
2. Independent market benchmark anchoring (USDA basis prices, CRB indexes, third-party broker quotes)
3. Documentation requirement for any non-market transactions
4. CCO consumes transfer price; does not set it
5. Audit trail with quarterly review by CFO/Audit Committee

### At GPG specifically

The scandal pattern maps directly to Craig Fields' seat in the Apr 29 reorg design — Craig governs both the merchant book (RPE) and owned supply (Tasteful + RPE contracted retail). The recommended governance overlay (Section 9.14 of the working doc) lands the transfer-pricing committee with Kirsten (CFO), not the CSCO whose scope is plant-operations only.

## When to Apply

- Any research touching RPE, merchant business models, brokerage operations
- Transfer pricing or intersegment economic governance
- M&A targets that have merchant + owned-supply hybrid models
- PE governance hygiene (especially exit-readiness analysis)
- Any commercial org design where one executive sits over both upstream and downstream of the same product flow

## Confidence

HIGH. SEC filings and public disclosure are definitive. ADM's restatement and settlement are matter of record. Use specific dates and dollar figures in any future analysis — they make the cautionary tale concrete and actionable rather than abstract.
